Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026  ·  CarcinoS LLC

CarcinoS LLC ("CarcinoS," "we," "us," or "our") respects your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your choices. It applies to our website, mobile application, and weekly digest service (collectively, the "Service").

Information We Collect

Category Examples How collected
Account information Name, email address, professional specialty, institution You provide it at signup
Preference data Selected disease sites, oncology scope, delivery preferences You set it in the signup flow or account settings
Device / push tokens Expo push token for mobile notifications Collected when you enable push notifications in the app
Usage data Pages visited, links clicked, digest open events Automatically via server logs and email analytics
Communications Emails you send to info@carcino-s.com You provide it

We do not collect patient data, protected health information (PHI), payment card numbers, or government identification numbers.

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Deliver your personalized weekly oncology digest via email and/or push notification;
  • Personalize content to your selected disease sites and oncology scope;
  • Operate, maintain, and improve the Service;
  • Communicate with you about your account, the Service, or our policies;
  • Detect and prevent fraud, abuse, or security incidents;
  • Comply with legal obligations.

We do not sell your personal information to third parties. We do not use your information to serve third-party advertising.

Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area, our legal bases for processing your personal data are:

  • Contract: Processing necessary to provide the Service you signed up for;
  • Legitimate interests: Improving the Service, preventing abuse, and communicating about material changes;
  • Consent: For marketing communications beyond the weekly digest you explicitly subscribed to.

Information Sharing

We share information only in these limited circumstances:

  • Service providers: We use Supabase (database hosting), Resend (email delivery), and Expo (push notifications) as infrastructure providers. Each is bound by data processing agreements and handles data only on our instructions.
  • Legal requirements: We may disclose information if required by law, subpoena, or to protect the rights or safety of CarcinoS, our users, or the public.
  • Business transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, user information may be transferred as part of that transaction, with advance notice to you.

Data Retention

We retain your account data for as long as your account is active. If you delete your account or unsubscribe, we will delete or anonymize your personal information within 90 days, except where we are required by law to retain it longer.

Security

We use industry-standard security measures including encrypted data transmission (TLS), server-side authentication, and row-level security in our database. However, no method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your location, you may have rights to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you;
  • Correct inaccurate information;
  • Delete your account and associated personal data;
  • Object to or restrict certain processing;
  • Port your data in a machine-readable format;
  • Opt out of marketing communications (every email includes an unsubscribe link).

To exercise any of these rights, email us at info@carcino-s.com. We will respond within 30 days.

California Residents (CCPA / CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act. We do not sell or share personal information as those terms are defined by the CCPA. To submit a request to know, delete, or opt out, contact info@carcino-s.com.

Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed to individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from minors. If you believe we have inadvertently collected information from a minor, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

Third-Party Links

The Service links to primary literature sources, PubMed, DOI resolvers, and publisher websites. These third-party sites have their own privacy policies, and we are not responsible for their practices. We encourage you to review their policies before providing any information.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will notify registered users by email and update the "Last updated" date above. Your continued use of the Service after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
